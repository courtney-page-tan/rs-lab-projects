IU Local Policy Map

AI & NLP 1,000+ Cities Municipal Data

The first large-scale database of local government policy priorities, built by collecting and analyzing publicly available municipal documents — budgets, city council minutes, and agendas — from over 1,000 Midwest cities using AI and NLP classification pipelines. The aim is to include all census-identified cities in the United States to understand patterns and changes in local policy priorities over time and space.

Disaster Media Database

A comprehensive database that collects, codes, and analyzes local news coverage of large-scale disasters. This searchable repository illuminates patterns in how disasters are reported and framed, supporting research in crisis communication, emergency management, institutional learning, and public perception of risk.

Media Analysis Crisis Communication

Climate Action Atlas

A collaborative, interactive dataset of real-world sustainability and resilience outcomes — documenting what worked, what didn't, and why. Researchers investigate case studies across energy, agriculture, infrastructure, and public health, contributing to a resource that helps communities and decision-makers learn from the strategies of others.

Sustainability Case Studies
☉

Portraits of Resilience

Using photovoice methodology, this project examines community risk and resilience through photography, culminating in a public exhibition. The study constructs a coded dataset linking participant perspectives with researcher observations to understand how residents perceive and navigate chronic stressors such as disinvestment, food insecurity, and institutional abandonment.

Photovoice Community Research
◆

Natural Hazards & Human Rights Violations

Investigates the relationship between natural hazards, climate change, and human rights violations, drawing on international legal frameworks and conflict event data. Combines legal analysis with empirical research to understand how disasters affect state repression, political violence, displacement, and other rights-related outcomes.

Human Rights Climate & Conflict
◗

Poisoning the Well? Water Security

Focuses on water security in Indiana and beyond, involving the collection of groundwater well measurement data for heavy metal contaminants — boron, lead, mercury, arsenic, cadmium, chromium, and selenium — sourced from the USGS and the Water Quality Portal.

Water Quality Environmental Health
